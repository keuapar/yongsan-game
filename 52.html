Gate 1

As you walk further down the street, you pass by a barbershop, a couple of photo studios, but mostly you see bars and restaurants. Surprisingly, the signs all appear both in Korean and English. The question of why is answered when you see shops replaced by a long brick wall topped with barbed wire. You pass by an entrance with the sign ‘GATE 1’ on a white plaque of a facility called ‘US Garrison’, and under that sign you see a girl with her back against the wall. She looks up at you.

Unsure of what to do, you wave back awkwardly. She responds by raising an eyebrow to you and pushes herself off the wall. “Hello,” she says casually.

“Can I help you?”

"No no. I was just passing through."

Park Soo-Geun (1914 - 1965) was a full-time painter and one of the most influential modernist. Although he didn’t formally receive art education in school, his skills won him numerous prizes. He predominantly used light gray color, to depict simple yet Korea-like themes in folklore sense.
