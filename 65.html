You trust her

“Tell you, what. I think I like you,” she finishes her cigarette and snuffs it out on the table before turning to you. “So, I’ll tell you what you probably haven’t guessed already. In a way, you and I are similar. I was taken off the streets too, but unlike you it was by choice when I met Mama.”

“Your mother?”

“Not by birth, but by choice. She protects me in a way that my family never could because she has power and money, all I have to do is see to her… clients, every once in awhile. I get to move around freely, have a small amount of income to do what I want. No one bothers me.”

“What do you do with her clients?”

“Provide some simple services. It really isn’t so bad. The soldiers after all get lonely sometimes,” she waves her hand dismissively. “But what that means is freedom.

Tell me girl, do you want to be free?”
