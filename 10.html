And so you stay

“I don’t know… I’m afraid…” you say tentatively.

“To end up like me? Don’t worry. I won’t make you leave. But you know it was good out there while it lasted,” a nostalgic feeling crosses over her face. “When I left here, I just ran straight all the way, hoping that someone would save me.

“I was running past a few factories and distinctly remember the smell of chocolate, so sweet and tempting, before I was caught,” she fades into silence for a moment. “And art, colourful art. It reminded me of home. You know my father is an artist and he always used to say that he wanted to come to Samgakji.”

You take a guess that she means that’s where we are at. “Why come here?”

“Because of Park Soo-Geun. His pieces are so original, I have never seen texture like that before on a painting. And he started right here painting portraits for soldiers and making copies of famous paintings. My father drew the most life-like portraits, as if they were moments from moving, but it felt too much like an imitation than the novel spin Park Soo-Geun put on his works. I wonder if my father is painting right now…” Her eyes become vacant, sadness welling them up.
