In the alley

You cross the street and walk into an alley. There are small shops lining the crooked alley. They shutters are down and the lights are turned off, but the smell of fish from their cooking still lingers in the air.

As you follow the winds of the alley, you notice that there is a shop with its light on which you find strange. You cautiously walk towards it and the sign says ‘BEST DAEGUTANG’. With your back against the wall you sneak a peek into the restaurant, careful not to be seen.

“Are you looking for something?” a voice asks from behind you.

You jump. Okay, your stealth mission was a fail. Can you try to play it cool and get that heart of yours to stop threatening to beat out of your chest? You become keenly aware of how you must look with your hair and clothes disheveled.

The voice was coming from a woman who looked like she was in her 60s. She is shorter than you, but her stern expression makes you feel smaller.

As you flounder and try to formulate a response, she raises an eyebrow and walks into her shop, telling you

“Come in. Quickly.”
