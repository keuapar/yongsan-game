Mama-san

“I know someone who can get you home,” she says. “Come with me.”

She leads you through the streets into a winding alley until you are in front of the gates of an apartment complex where there is strangely only one room with its lights on. You follow her in and she closes the gate behind you. You continue to follow her up the stairs to a dimly lit corridor with yellow walls and doors that all look the same. She makes a few more turns and climbs up a couple more flights of stairs before stopping in front of a room.

*Knock* *knock* *pause* *knock*.

A beautiful older woman opens the door and you are greeted by the smell of incense and a cosy living room with colourful pillows. A tea set sits as the centrepiece of the room with ornate oriental patterns. The woman walks back into the room without acknowledging either of you.

“Is this your house?” you ask quietly.

“More like Mama’s establishment,” she says. “Mama, I brought you something.”

“Come in and take a seat,” Mama beckons to you before turning her attention to the other girl. “You may leave.”

As she leaves and you settle on the couch, she sits beside you and asks

“What brings you here this night?”
